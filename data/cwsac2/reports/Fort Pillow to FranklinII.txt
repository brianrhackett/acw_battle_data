Update to the Civil War Sites Advisory Commission Report on the Nation’s Civil War Battlefields
Final DRAFT -- State of Tennessee

74

Condition Statement

The battlefield has been transformed from rural to suburban land.
Only previously protected areas remain. In an effort to provide
visitors with a better sense of the battlefield’s historic setting, the
City of Franklin has taken a series of actions to reclaim, restore,
and reconstruct portions of disturbed battlefield for interpretive
purposes.

Historical Designation

National Historic Landmark, Franklin Battlefield (1966), consists of
the following four non-contiguous properties: Carter House, Fort
Granger, Winstead Hill, and Carnton and the McGavock Cemetery.
In addition, the following sites are listed independently on the
National Register of Historic Places: Harrison House (1975), Roper’s
Knob Fortifications (2000) and the Alpheus Truett House (1988).
While these isolated sites are located within the Study Area, the
battlefield landscape is not listed in the National Register.
