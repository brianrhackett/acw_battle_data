Drewry’s Bluff (VA012)
Location

Chesterfield County

Campaign

Peninsula Campaign (March-September 1862)

Battle Date(s)

May 15, 1862

Principal Commanders

Cdr. John Rodgers [US]; Cdr. E. Farrand, Brig. Gen. William
Mahone, Capt. S. S. Lee, and Lt. John Taylor Wood [CS]

Forces Engaged

Gunboats, 5 [US]; Battery Garrison, unknown [CS]

Results

Confederate victory

Study Area

564.83 acres
The CWSAC boundary incorrectly identified actions and lands on the
south side of the James River as associated with the May 15, 1862,
US naval attack on the CSA fortifications at Drewry’s Bluff. The
revised Study Area is limited to the portion of the works and troop
positions on the bluffs, and the stretch of river within accurate
range of the Confederate guns where the Federal flotilla
maneuvered and anchored during the engagement.
